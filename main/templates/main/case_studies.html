Representative Case Examples

Wrongful Death

Construction Site Fatality Economic Analysis

Case Type: Wrongful death involving a 42-year-old construction foreman in a crane collapse incident.

Factors Analyzed: Work-life expectancy, career progression patterns, union benefit structures, household services contributions, and employer-provided benefits including health insurance and retirement contributions.

Methodology Applied: Human capital approach for earnings capacity, replacement cost method for household services, and actuarial analysis for benefit valuations. Incorporated Bureau of Labor Statistics data and industry-specific wage growth projections.

Note: Case details modified to protect client confidentiality

Business Valuation

Restaurant Chain Breach of Contract Analysis

Case Type: Commercial litigation involving multi-location restaurant franchise and territorial exclusivity agreement breach.

Factors Analyzed: Historical financial performance, market share data, competitive landscape, customer traffic patterns, and comparable restaurant performance metrics across similar territories.

Methodology Applied: "But-for" analysis comparing actual versus projected performance, regression analysis of sales drivers, and market penetration modeling. Examined five years of financial data with seasonality adjustments.

Note: Case details modified to protect client confidentiality

Personal Injury

Traumatic Brain Injury - Technology Professional

Case Type: Personal injury involving a 28-year-old software engineer with traumatic brain injury from motor vehicle accident.

Factors Analyzed: Pre-injury career trajectory, cognitive function impact on earning capacity, technology sector advancement patterns, stock option vesting schedules, and vocational rehabilitation possibilities.

Methodology Applied: Differential earnings analysis comparing pre-injury and post-injury earning capacity. Collaboration with vocational experts to assess functional limitations. Analysis of technology industry compensation surveys and career progression data.

Note: Case details modified to protect client confidentiality

Medical Malpractice

Birth Injury Life Care Planning

Case Type: Medical malpractice involving birth-related injuries resulting in cerebral palsy diagnosis.

Factors Analyzed: Medical care requirements, therapy needs (physical, occupational, speech), assistive technology, home modifications, educational support services, and attendant care requirements through life expectancy.

Methodology Applied: Life care planning methodology incorporating medical expert opinions, published medical cost databases, geographic cost variations, and medical inflation projections. Present value calculations using appropriate discount rates per jurisdiction.

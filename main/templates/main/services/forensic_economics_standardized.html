Frequently Asked Questions

A forensic economist is an expert who calculates economic damages in legal cases, including lost earnings, future medical costs, and business losses. They provide expert testimony and detailed economic analysis for personal injury, wrongful death, and commercial litigation cases.

Lost earnings are calculated by analyzing past earnings history, future earning capacity, work-life expectancy, fringe benefits, and applying appropriate growth rates and discount rates to determine present value. This includes both past lost wages and future earning capacity losses.

Forensic economists are needed for personal injury, wrongful death, medical malpractice, employment discrimination, wrongful termination, business interruption, breach of contract, and any case requiring economic damage calculations.

Forensic economist fees vary based on case complexity, typically ranging from $250-$500 per hour for analysis and $500-$1000 per hour for testimony. Most cases require 20-40 hours of work. Contact us for a specific quote based on your case needs.

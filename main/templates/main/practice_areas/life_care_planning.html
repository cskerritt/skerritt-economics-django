Expert Life Care Planning Services

As a Certified Life Care Planner (CLCP), Chris Skerritt develops comprehensive life care plans that project future medical and non-medical needs for individuals with catastrophic injuries and chronic conditions. Our evidence-based plans provide attorneys with detailed cost projections essential for securing appropriate compensation.

Core Life Care Planning Services

  • Comprehensive Medical Needs Assessment: Detailed evaluation of all future medical requirements
  • Cost Projections: Itemized pricing for all medical and non-medical needs
  • Physician Collaboration: Coordination with treating physicians for recommendations
  • Home Modifications: Assessment and costing of necessary accessibility modifications
  • Assistive Technology: Evaluation of equipment and technology needs
  • Attendant Care: Quantification of personal care assistance requirements
  • Transportation Needs: Vehicle modifications and medical transportation costs
  • Complications Planning: Contingencies for potential medical complications

Our Life Care Planning Process

  1. Medical Records Review: Comprehensive analysis of all medical documentation
  2. Client Assessment: When appropriate, in-person or virtual evaluation
  3. Physician Consultation: Collaboration with treating physicians for recommendations
  4. Research & Costing: Current pricing research for all plan components
  5. Plan Development: Creation of detailed, time-based care plan
  6. Economic Analysis: Present value calculations and inflation adjustments
  7. Report Preparation: Comprehensive report with detailed cost tables
  8. Expert Testimony: Deposition and trial testimony support

Life Care Plan Components

Comprehensive analysis typically includes assessment of:

  • Spinal Cord Injury (Quadriplegia): 24-hour attendant care, adaptive equipment, home modifications, and specialized medical management
  • Spinal Cord Injury (Paraplegia): Mobility devices, accessible housing, and ongoing rehabilitation services
  • Severe Traumatic Brain Injury: Cognitive rehabilitation, behavioral support, and long-term care facility options
  • Birth Injury with CP: Developmental therapies, educational support, and lifetime medical management
  • Severe Burns (>40% TBSA): Reconstructive surgeries, compression garments, and psychological support
  • Multiple Amputations: Prosthetic devices, occupational therapy, and vocational rehabilitation

*Each plan is individualized based on medical assessment, functional capacity, and specific needs
